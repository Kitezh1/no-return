NO RETURN: THE FIRST FALL

Chapter One: The Fall

Posted on June 14th, 2023

The escape pod jolted violently as it hurtled through the atmosphere, carrying Nikki, Mal, and Harris. The deafening roar of the crashing spaceship echoed in their ears. Their eyes widened as they exchanged glances, unsure of what awaited them upon impact. Moments later, the pod crash-landed, the force of the impact shaking them to their core.

Battered and disoriented, the trio emerged from the pod, surveying their surroundings. They found themselves in the heart of a dense rainforest, surrounded by towering trees and an eerie silence. The pungent scent of damp earth and foliage hung in the air.

"We made it," Nikki murmured, her voice filled with a mix of relief and trepidation. She ran her fingers through her tousled blond hair, her blue eyes scanning the unfamiliar terrain.

Mal's scientist instincts kicked in as she started exploring their immediate vicinity. With a wry smile on her face, she quipped, "Well, isn't this a delightful mess we find ourselves in?" Her humor, albeit tinged with uncertainty, helped ease the tension.

Harris, ever the stoic soldier, took a deep breath, his eyes darting around, scanning for potential threats. "Let's gather what supplies we have left and find a suitable place to set up camp for the night," he suggested.

The trio scoured the wreckage of the pod, salvaging useful items—water bottles, a few packets of food, a knife, and some rope. It was a meager collection, but they knew they had to make do. As the sun dipped below the canopy, they stumbled upon a clearing where they could construct a temporary shelter.

Using their limited resources and their combined survival skills, they set to work. They wove sturdy branches together, securing them with the rope, and fashioned a simple lean-to shelter. It wouldn't withstand a torrential downpour, but it would provide some respite for the night.

Exhausted but grateful for their makeshift abode, the trio gathered around a crackling campfire, the warmth providing solace in the darkness. They ate the last of their rations, savoring each bite as they shared tales of their lives before the crash.

Nikki reminisced about her days as a chef, describing the joy she found in creating culinary wonders for others. Mal regaled them with stories of her scientific endeavors, recounting the thrill of unlocking secrets hidden within the universe. Harris remained reticent, revealing little of his past, but his presence and strength reassured the others.

Days turned into nights, and time lost its grip on their fragile existence in the rainforest. They foraged for edible plants and searched for fresh water, learning to adapt to the unforgiving environment. Each night, they would gather around the campfire, continuing their tradition of sharing stories and lending support to one another.

As dusk settled on another weary day, a sense of unease tingled in the air. The crackling of twigs broke the tranquility, catching their attention. The trio tensed as a mysterious figure emerged from the shadows, steadily approaching their campfire.

Their eyes locked onto the stranger, uncertainty etched on their faces, as the first chapter came to an end.
