NO RETURN: THE FIRST FALL

Chapter Two: Encounters & Warnings

Posted on June 15th, 2023

Nikki, Mal, and Harris sat around their makeshift campfire, exchanging stories and recounting the terrifying crash that brought them to this treacherous rainforest. The crackling flames danced in the night, casting an eerie glow upon their faces.

In the midst of their conversation, a figure emerged from the shadows, cautiously approaching their camp. Nikki's friendly demeanor remained intact as she welcomed the stranger with a warm smile. Mal, ever curious, regarded the newcomer with a glimmer of intrigue. Harris, however, maintained a skeptical glance, his eyes never leaving the mysterious man.

The stranger introduced himself as Malin, hailing from a tribe known as the Red Caco. His weathered face bore the marks of wisdom and survival. As they conversed, Malin shared insights about the planet they were stranded on—how it was ruthless and unforgiving, concealing hidden dangers in every corner.

As the conversation unfolded, Malin's eyes met Nikki's hopeful gaze as she innocently asked if they could join his tribe. A hint of regret clouded his expression as he explained that the Red Caco did not welcome new visitors. He offered them a small token of friendship, presenting them with a bundle of medical supplies, emphasizing the importance of being prepared for the perils ahead. With a farewell, he swiftly retreated into the darkness, leaving the group pondering their next move.

As night settled, a sense of unease permeated the camp. Suddenly, a bloodcurdling screech shattered the silence, and Mal found herself under attack by a ferocious, enraged rat. Acting swiftly, she reached for her revolver, firing a precise shot that repelled the creature. The group gathered around the defeated rodent, its lifeless form serving as a grim reminder of the dangers lurking in this unfamiliar realm.

Mal, drawing upon her scientific background, examined the wound inflicted by the rat. Her eyes widened with concern as she observed a peculiar sight—the wound oozed a purple-colored liquid. Expressing her hypothesis, she warned her companions that certain animals or even humans on this planet might possess toxins they were unfamiliar with. Hunting for food became a treacherous endeavor, and they needed to exercise utmost caution if they were to sustain themselves beyond the limited resources of plants and berries.

With newfound awareness, the trio understood the gravity of their situation. Survival would demand more than resourcefulness—it would require a meticulous understanding of the flora and fauna that could provide sustenance without inflicting harm. The rainforest had become their battleground, and they vowed to be both cunning and cautious in their pursuit of sustenance.

As the night pressed on, their campfire crackled, casting flickering shadows upon their resolute faces. They huddled closer, devising a plan to navigate this merciless terrain, their spirits undeterred by the perils that awaited them.

And so, the second chapter came to an end, leaving them poised to face the formidable challenges that lay ahead.
