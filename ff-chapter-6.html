NO RETURN: THE FIRST FALL

Chapter Six: Seeking Solutions

Posted on June 20th, 2023

The following day arrived, casting its dim light into the cave where Mal and Nikki were still fast asleep. Outside, Harris sat by the flickering fire, his watchful eyes scanning the surroundings. His shift as the night guard would soon come to an end, but thoughts of their predicament weighed heavily on his mind.

Reflecting on their isolated existence, Harris pondered the possibility of venturing further in search of help or a way back home. The relentless desire for answers gnawed at his spirit, pushing him to consider exploring beyond their current boundaries. As the crackling fire provided a backdrop for his thoughts, he found solace in the notion of taking action.

Suddenly, the sound of footsteps approached from behind. Nikki settled down beside him, her presence comforting in the quiet solitude of the night. She offered to take over the watch, allowing him some respite before they continued their conversation.

"I can see it in your eyes, Harris," Nikki spoke softly, breaking the silence. "You're thinking about going out there, aren't you? To find help or some kind of solution."

Harris nodded, grateful for her understanding. "Yeah, Nikki. We can't just sit here forever, hoping for a miracle. We need to take action, take control of our fate."

The night pressed on, their hushed conversation intertwining with the gentle crackling of the fire. As their talk came to an end, Harris bid Nikki farewell and retreated into the cave, preparing to rest beside Mal.

But just as he was about to lay down, a thunderous crash resonated through the air, shaking the ground beneath their feet. Instinctively, all three of them sprang forward, their hearts pounding with a mixture of curiosity and apprehension. They followed the trail of smoke in the distance, arming themselves with weapons as they pressed forward.

Passing through the dense trees, their eyes fell upon a familiar sight—a crashed pod, similar to the one they had escaped from. The door of the pod was sealed shut, fueling their sense of anticipation. Harris and Mal worked together, opening a side panel to unlock the door, while Nikki kept watch, alert for any potential danger.

With a hiss, the door swung open, revealing a hollow interior. Disappointment flooded their hearts as they realized the pod was empty, save for one lifeless body seated in the driver's seat. Mal carefully removed the helmet, revealing the face of a tanned woman with dark hair. Harris recognized her as one of the soldiers from a recent training class he had overseen.

After deliberation, they decided to bury the woman, paying their respects to the fallen soldier. Though her name escaped Harris's memory, he dug a grave and adorned it with flowers. Sitting by the makeshift grave, he remained for a while, allowing his thoughts to drift before eventually rejoining Nikki and Mal back at camp.

Mal engaged in conversation with Nikki, contemplating the implications of the newly discovered crashed pod. "If the crash pods are still coming down," Mal mused, "then the main ship must still be active. There might be others out there."

Meanwhile, Harris joined the others in the cave, explaining his plan for the following morning. He declared his intention to venture further in search of help, aware that his decision might not sit well with his companions. Assuring them that they were capable and equipped to handle themselves, he emphasized their knowledge of firearms.

Nikki stood up, her eyes filled with understanding. "I get it," she said, her voice resolute. "We need to do something. Rest up, both of you. Tomorrow will be a new day."

With her words hanging in the air, Nikki settled herself by the campfire, taking up the role of the night watch once more. As she stared into the dancing flames, her mind was consumed with the burning question of how they would escape this seemingly insurmountable situation.
