NO RETURN: THE FIRST FALL

Chapter Four: The Rescue

Posted on June 17th, 2023

Harris trudged through the dense undergrowth, the weight of disappointment hanging over him. The two small turtles he had managed to catch would barely provide a satisfying meal for the group, but he knew they had to make do with what they had. With a heavy sigh, he began his journey back to camp, unaware of the imminent danger that awaited him.

As he approached the camp, a flickering glow caught Harris's attention. His heart sank as he realized the fire was blazing uncontrollably and his companions, Mal and Nikki, were nowhere to be seen. Instinctively, he crouched behind a cluster of trees, his mind racing to analyze the situation before making a move.

Peering through the foliage, Harris observed two men rummaging through their bags. They were armed with bows, their intentions unclear. Straining his ears, he strained to catch snippets of conversation, but Nikki's words were muffled and incomprehensible. Frustration surged through him as one of the men shouted at Nikki, his bow aimed menacingly in her direction.

Time seemed to slow as Harris's training kicked in. He knew he had to act swiftly to protect his friends. Steadying his aim, he shouldered his rifle and squeezed the trigger. The crack of the gunshot shattered the stillness of the forest as the bullet found its mark—a clean shot to the head. The man crumpled to the ground, lifeless.

The remaining assailant, startled by the sudden turn of events, darted behind a nearby tree, seeking refuge from Harris's accurate shot. The fire continued to rage, casting an eerie glow upon the chaotic scene. From his vantage point, Harris watched as Nikki seized the opportunity, grabbing the fallen man's bow and positioning herself protectively over Mal's unconscious form.

Without hesitation, the second man, fueled by fear and desperation, sprinted towards the cover of the surrounding foliage. Harris's finger tightened around the trigger once more, but his shot missed its target, striking a tree instead. The man disappeared into the underbrush, leaving Harris with a sense of urgency to reach his vulnerable companions.

Rushing down from his concealed position, Harris closed the distance between himself and the camp. Concern etched across his face, he called out to Nikki, his voice filled with determination and relief. "Nikki! Are you both alright?" he shouted, his words echoing through the night.

Nikki, her eyes filled with a mixture of relief and caution, nodded, clutching the bow tightly. "We're okay, Harris. They caught us by surprise, but we managed to hold our ground," she replied, her voice laced with resilience.

Together, they assessed the situation. The fire raged on, threatening to consume what little remained of their shelter. They hurriedly gathered their meager belongings, salvaging what they could before the flames claimed them. Harris's sharp gaze scanned the surroundings, searching for any sign of the fleeing intruder.

With the immediate threat neutralized, Harris turned his attention to Mal, whose unconscious form lay still. Tenderly, he knelt beside her, checking her pulse and assessing her condition. "She's alive, but we need to get her back to camp and tend to her wounds," he said, his voice filled with concern.

With the immediate threat neutralized, Harris turned his attention to Mal, whose unconscious form lay still. Tenderly, he knelt beside her, checking her pulse and assessing her condition. "She's alive, but we need to get her back to camp and tend to her wounds," he said, his voice filled with concern.

Shouldering their remaining supplies, the trio left the chaotic scene behind, making their way back to the campsite. The night air was heavy with tension and unanswered questions. As they reached the remnants of their once-safe haven, they realized it was no longer a suitable place to stay.

"We can't stay here," Nikki voiced the growing unease. "It's too dangerous, and whoever these men were, they could comeback with more people."

Harris nodded in agreement, his gaze scanning the surroundings. "We need to find a new location, somewhere more defensible, where we can establish a safer camp," he suggested, determination gleaming in his eyes.

The group packed their remaining belongings, leaving behind the charred remains of their makeshift shelter. Their footsteps echoed through the night as they ventured deeper into the unfamiliar terrain, seeking a place where they could rebuild their sanctuary. The dangers of this untamed planet were becoming more evident with each passing moment, but their camaraderie propelled them forward.

As they disappeared into the darkness, a sense of hope mingled with the uncertainty. They were determined to face whatever challenges lay ahead, united in their shared goal of survival.
